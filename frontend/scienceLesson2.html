Heat - Expansion and Contraction

We will focus on the following two main aspects:

  • Expansion
  • Contraction

    • Expansion

  • When object is heated, an increase of temperature takes place and will expand
  • a change of state may occur depends how hot the object is
  • For example, as solid ice gains heat, it will reach above 0 degrees and melt to form liquid water.
  • no matter what state the object is, it will expand if heat is gained

    • Contraction

  • When object is cooled, a decrease of temperature takes place and will contract
  • a change of state may occur depends how cold the object is
  • For example, as water loses heat, it will reach below 0 degrees and freeze to form ice
  • no matter what state the object is, it will contract if heat is lost
