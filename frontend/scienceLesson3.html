Human Digestive System

The human digestive system is made up of the mouth, gullet, stomach, small intestine, large intestine and anus.
The main function of the human digestive system is to:

  • break down (digest) food into simpler substances
    for the body to absorb and use.

    • Top Digestive Sytem

  • In the mouth, there digestive fluids in the saliva to
    digest the food and our teeth are to break the food down even further
  • In the gullet, the muscles help push the digested food into the stomach. There are no digestive fluids hence food is not digested in gullet
  • In the stomach, it produces gastric acid like digestive juices & acid.
    The juices break down the food into simpler substances while the acid kills bacteria

    • Bottom Digestive Sytem

  • In the small intestine, most of the food will be digested here and digestion will also be stopped here.
    Digested food will be then absorbed by the walls of intestine and the rest of the undigested food will be sent to the large intestine
  • In the large intestine, the main function is to absorb excess water from the undigested food and passed out as faeces through anus
